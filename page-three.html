THE GOSPEL ACCORDING TO ST MARK


The Book of Mark



"And Barnabas determined to take with them John, whose surname was Mark" -Acts 15:37


Who was Mark?


Mark is often referred to as the companion of Peter and also Paul. Mark is mentioned a few times in the New Testament. Some of the main ones are:

  1. 1 Peter 5:13
  2. Acts 12:25
  3. Philemon 1:24

He is known as being the writer for Peter, so he would have heard many stories of Christ directly from someone who knew Him.

As his account is the oldest one that we have it is likely that he knew Christ when He was still on the earth. He may even be the young man who flees from Gethsemane in Mark 14.


To whom does the Book of Mark address?


Mark was a Jew. But his writting makes it clear that he is addressing Gentiles in the West. His knowledge of Roman coinage makes it possible that he was addresssing Romans. But we can see that he is writting to Gentiles with how much detail he uses to explain Jewish traditions.


What do we learn from the Book of Mark?


A big message of the Book of Mark is how much Jesus suffered. His depiction of Christ makes Him seem the most human of all the other Gospels. His is the most narrative description of Christ's life so we can learn a lot about the actual story of Christ.

From Mark we really feel the story of Christ. As we read it let us remember how much he suffered for us.
