THE GOSPEL ACCORDING TO ST MATTHEW


The Book of Matthew



"And as Jesus passed forth from thence, he saw a man, named Matthew" -Matthew 9:9


Who was Matthew?


Matthew the Evangelist was one of Christ's original Apostles. Because he was one of the original Apostles he shows up in many places in the Gospels. Like:

  1. Matthew 9:9
  2. Matthew 10:3
  3. Mark 2:14

Matthew has a unique view point on the life of Christ as he is one of the Apostles. He was a tax collector originally until Christ saw him sitting by the customs house in Capernaum.

Perhaps it's because he was a tax collector but his account is the most structured of them all.


To whom does the Book of Matthew address?


The Book of Matthew was written for a Jewish audience. Especially those early converts who were Jewish. Because of this, Christ in this account was the most "Jewish" than in all of the other accounts.


What do we learn from the Book of Matthew?


Because it was written for a Jewish audience, a big theme in this book is how Jesus is and was the Jewish Messiah. A lot of Jews were waiting for someone to come and free them from the reign of the Romans.

However, Matthew reminds us that Christ frees us from things much more important than empires of the earth. He frees us from sin because He died for us.
